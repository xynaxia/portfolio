How might we let people interact face to face safely, in times of covid-19?

Company: Cognizant/Mirabeau

Project Managers: Peet Sneekes, Hayo Rubingh

Project Type: Graduation Project, IoT, Smart Buildings

Project Duration: 28 weeks

Responsibilities: UX Research, Concepting, Design, Prototyping

The Product

Pavillion is a social network based on smart-office technology. The core concept is bringing people together safely, by using gathering points. These gathering points each have their own function. They can be work related, such as finding someone with specific knowledge. But also informal, such as finding someone to take a walk outside.

Background

Offices are pleasant spaces that facilitate focussed work, warm social encounters, engaging collaborations and inspiration. Offices are also very intuitive, socially driven and tactile spaces. So, how can people behave intuitively and comfortably and still make sure they stay healthy when viral infection is more likely than before?

The people I designed for are the employees in the office of Mirabeau. Those who come to the office to seek social interaction with their co-workers. This means I did not design for those saying home (remote workers). Below you can see a persona that tells more about this. I have not included a picture, nor an age or gender, because this information wasn’t relevant for my case.

The Current Situation

Researching and analysing research is what I most enjoy about a project. To get a good idea of the current situation I have done extensive UX research with over twenty user interviews, user shadowing and tons of desk research I collected massive amounts of data. One of the problems with doing so much research is drowning in my own data...

Ensuring my insights are sharable, I’ve clustered the interviews through qualitative coding, turned the themes into clusters, formulated insight statements, further researched these insights to validate them and shared them with my project managers turning them into actions through design principles.

Brainstorming

To generate ideas my first action was to find a lot of inspiration. For bringing people together, I’ve looked at social platforms. The most interesting thing I found here was that people were brought together through the use of events. With this in mind I organised a brainstorming session with three other people, to think of how this could apply to offices.

Another point of interest was spontaneity. This was a paradox… Needing to control and manage space, while at the same time stimulating free interaction. Here I took inspiration from other technology, a big one was a smart toilet in shanghai used for decreasing the waiting queues at the toilet. What was especially interesting here was that the technology was used to guide people, rather than controlling where they go. This was a principle I took with me when I designed my own smart solution.

Killing my darling

Eventually I was left with three concepts… These concepts were presented to the case owners and shared with the people to get some external feedback. The first concept I went for was coughy. Coughy was a smart coffee machine that attempted to nudge people into keeping distance.

Coughy had a face and displayed emotions based on sensor data. He would get worried when it got too busy and stare at people who were standing too close. The data for measuring this were thermal sensors.

However, as fun as the concept was. I decided to kill my darling. This concept was covid centered, not human centered. I got too excited because the idea sounded funny, but from a people perspective it was very gimmicky.
